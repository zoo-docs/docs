## 2. Multilateral Frameworks

Zoo aligns with and reports against the following frameworks
(ZIP-0550, Conservation Standards Alignment):

- **IUCN Red List** — species classification source of truth.
- **CITES** — Appendices I/II/III for trade-status verification.
- **Global Biodiversity Framework** — 2030 targets.
- **GBIF** — open biodiversity data publication standard
  (ZIP-0601).
- **WWF / WCS / ZSL / Panthera** — field-operations partners.

## 6. Partnership Framework

ZIP-0530 (Community Partnerships, FPIC) is the canonical framework
for partnership intake. Every named partnership requires:

1. Free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) of the local community
   for in-situ partnerships.
2. Public registration on the Partnership Registry (Zoo L1 contract).
3. Quarterly review of impact metrics under ZIP-0501.
4. Sunset clauses with renewal vote (DAO).
5. Conflict-of-interest disclosure (DAO board members and
   contributors).

## 7. The Power of Decentralization (§25)

The 2021 paper's closing line on partnerships:

> *"The power of decentralization."*

We honour this by: never accepting exclusive partnerships that bind
the protocol; never paywalling protocol features to a partner's
infrastructure; always publishing partner data through the Open
Biodiversity Database Standard (ZIP-0601). Partners are amplifiers,
not gatekeepers.
